# What are the impacts of GEN AI on your brain?

A [study by the National Library of Medicine (NIH) from the USA](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12255134/) states that generative AI is a very useful tool to enhance productivity. However, concerns are raised that the use of generative AI may erode human cognition due to over-reliance.

On the other hand others argue that generative AI holds the promise to improve human cognition by automating menial tasks.

Nowadays with the increased usage of AI, many people begin to rely too much on it and in extreme scenarios use it to answer very simple questions that require little critical thinking or even use it to ask "what they have learned during the day" which is impossible to answer without enough context.

All of this leads to the degradation of the individuals critical thinking skills

# How is genAI impacting the job market of junior positions in the last years?

Based on research by Rabobank researchers as written in a statement in the economic journal by [ESB](https://esb.nu/dalende-werkgelegenheid-onder-nederlandse-jongeren-die-concurreren-met-genai/), CBS employment figures show that employment for young people aged 15 to 24 has declined sharply in GenAI-eligible occupational groups. In the third quarter of 2025, over 13 percent fewer young people were working in these groups than in the fourth quarter of 2022. In the other occupational groups, youth employment rose by 3 percent. The gap is much smaller for other age groups.

# How do you plan to develop your core knowledge and skills?

I plan to use AI strategically as a learning tool, not a crutch. Specifically, I use it to clarify concepts I don't understand, validate my design thinking, and structure documentation professionally. However, I ensure I understand the "why" behind every decision. I will NOT use AI to skip the hard thinking or pretend knowledge I don't have. The goal is to enhance my critical thinking by automating the routine parts so I can focus mental effort on the technical decisions that matter. This means I'm more likely to develop deeper expertise, not less.
