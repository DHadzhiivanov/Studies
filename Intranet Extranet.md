## What is the Difference?

**Intranet** is used to describe a private connection of LANs and WANs that belongs to an organization. It is designed only to be accessible by the organization's members, employees, or others with authorization.

An organization may use an **extranet** to provide secure and safe access to individuals who work for for a different organization but require access to the organization's data.

Some examples of an extranet include:

- Google sharing user data with companies for promotional purposes or the other way around
- Hospitals that provide booking systems to doctors so they can make appointments for their patients
- A company that is providing access to outside suppliers or contractors
