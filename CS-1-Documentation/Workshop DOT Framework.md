# 1. What is Research

Research can be defined as the act of creating new knowledge or learning new knowledge to apply it.

---

# 2. Reason for Research

The reason is to find solutions to immediate problems and focus on the product.

The outcome is always an **anser** or/and a **solution**.

It is not a separated task, but a way to achieve things systematically using existing technology and methods.

---

# 3. Possible Research

- Investigating Stakeholder Wishes
- Choosing most appropriate technology
- Investigating the usefulnes of technologies and comparing them
- Validating your decision

---

# 4. DOT Setup

Development Oriented Triangulation.

- What?
- Why?
- How

The key is to research from multiple sources and find the best solution. Never stick to one solution but always consider different ones from different sources.

Research Methods:

- Library
- Field
	- Comparing products
	- Getting info from different sources
- Lab
	- Doing tests yourself
- Showroom
	- Showcase your product
		- Do a pitch like you do in Show & Grow
		- Get reviews from peers / experts
- Workshop
	- Prototyping
	- Brainstorming

Another key is to be AWARE of your research.

---

# 5. How to Formulate Questions

- Choose your topic
- Do some preliminary reading about the current state of the field
- Narrow your focus to a specific niche
- Identify the research question

## 5.1 Relevance

Does it align with the main goal?

## 5.2 Neutrality

Is it unbiased and open-ended?

- Avoid sneaking solutions

## 5.3 Open-endedness

Does it invite exploration, not a yes/no?

- Aim for depth

## 5.4 Functionality

Does it help understand the problem or guide the solution?

- Must clearly serve the professional purpose.

## 5.5 Feasibility

Can you realistically answer it within available time and resources?

- Don't over-commit

## 5.6 Clarity

Is the question understandable and clear?

- Be precise
