# Reading Guide

Each portfolio is defined by its **reading guide**. This is the **entrypoint to each learning outcome** and is where you connect all evidence you uploaded over the semester together to tell the story of your learning progress. In it, you describe for each individual learning outcome what it means to you and how you intent to demonstrate this leanring outcome. It includes a **self-assessment** and **reflection** on your challenges, strenghts and weaknesses while working on the learning outcome, and you provide a **list of links to your various uploaded evidence** to back up your claim.

### Checklist

Since the reading guide is starting point of your portfolio and describes the full semester worth of your work to prove you demonstrated the learnign outcomes, you can use the checklist below to assess if the Reading Guide meets **basic quality standards**.  

- **Spelling and grammar**: All text is checked for spelling and grammar errors
    
- **Readability**: Text is readable, understandable and language is of a professional standard
    
- **Evidence**: The evidence list and professional products match the VRAAKT Criteria:
    
    - **Voldoende (Sufficient):** The evidence includes enough detail or depth to clearly demonstrate your skills or learning.
    - **Relevant (Relevant):** The evidence directly relates to one or more learning outcomes and supports your progress.
    - **Authentiek (Authentic):** The work is genuinely your own and represents real, meaningful learning or contribution.
    - **Actueel (Current):** The evidence reflects your most recent and up-to-date knowledge or skills.
    - **Kwantitatief (Quantitative):** The amount of evidence is appropriate—neither too little nor excessive—for what you're trying to prove.
    - **Traceerbaar (Traceable):** The evidence is clearly documented and easy to understand, with enough context to show when and how it was created.
    
- **References:** Sources are referenced in APA-style, citations are correctly attributed to the original author
